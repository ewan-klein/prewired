Progressive enhancement

This is a way to make websites that are usable by as many people as possible, by starting with the simplest version and building up in layers.

Parts of progressive enhancement include:

  • Responsive web design

  • Mobile-first development

  • Accessibility

This is an area that is still under development and lots of people write (and argue) about it all the time. I'll find some good blogs for you to follow about it at some point...

Layers of front-end development

  1. HTML (semantic & accessible html)

    • Semantic markup is using the right tags for the right things. See this list of HTML5 elements.

    • Accessible means making the content available for people who use websites in different ways (like blind people, people who can't use a mouse, people with really rubbish phones, and other things). There are lots of really easy ways to make your markup more accessible.

  2. CSS

    • You could use a CSS Reset to normalise everything across different browsers.

    • Do CSS in an 'object-oriented' way. Use generic, re-usable classes (.classname) to make understanding and changing things much easier.

    • Try google.com/webfonts for loads of good fonts

    • Do your fonts, colours and stuff first.

    • THEN do layout.

    • Think about responsive web design! (Media queries).
